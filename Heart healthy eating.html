The Complete Guide to Heart-Healthy Eating

Evidence-based nutrition strategies to protect your cardiovascular system

Why Heart Health Starts in Your Kitchen

Research shows that 80% of heart disease is preventable through dietary choices. The foods you eat directly impact:

  • Blood pressure levels
  • Cholesterol profiles
  • Inflammation markers
  • Artery function

The Heart-Healthy Plate Formula

50% Vegetables & Fruits

Rich in potassium, fiber, and antioxidants

25% Lean Proteins

Fish, poultry, legumes, and plant-based proteins

25% Whole Grains

Oats, quinoa, brown rice, and whole wheat

Top 10 Heart-Protective Foods

  1. Fatty Fish

    Salmon, mackerel, and sardines provide omega-3s that reduce inflammation and triglycerides. Aim for 2 servings/week.

  2. Oats and Barley

    Rich in beta-glucan fiber that lowers LDL cholesterol by 5-10%.

  3. Leafy Greens

    Spinach, kale, and collards are packed with vitamin K, which protects arteries.

  4. Berries

    Anthocyanins in blueberries and strawberries reduce blood pressure.

  5. Walnuts

    Contain ALA omega-3s that improve arterial function.

Expert Tip

"Instead of focusing on eliminating 'bad' foods, concentrate on adding heart-protective foods. Just adding 1-2 servings of berries or leafy greens daily can make a significant difference." - Dr. Sarah Johnson, Cardiologist
